Spirit

Sprouts of Projects: Where abandoned projects might find a new life!

Instead of letting memories of old abandoned projects collect dust, we shall put them here for our community to see. Maybe someone else can pick it up, or take your old idea as inspiration. We should not be afraid to present unfinished ideas because they did not attain perfection! It is good to remember and cherish the motivations we once had. So many neuroqueers probably have stacks of half-baked projects. Let us remember them!

If you have a project you wish to add to this collection, feel free to email the editrix. Her email address is on the main page of this website.

Tallyball

from the Editrix

This is a sport I invented with my brother during our vacations from school. It is played with two teams and a ball. We used a tennis ball. The main point of the game is that there are many ways to score, with riskier ways rewarded with more points. The ways to score came from basketball, baseball, rugby, volleyball, bowling, and other ball sports. I imagined two teams playing this in a racquetball court. Which ways to score would they resort to more? I imagined adjusting the number of points for each method of scoring during a trial period of observing two teams playing tallyball, so that the players' motivations to use each method would become even, meaning they would find it as worthwhile to use any method as any other method. Then the game would look quite varied and interesting.
